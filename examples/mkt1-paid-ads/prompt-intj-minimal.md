Create a minimalist editorial infographic in portrait 3:4 ratio.

## Visual Style
Editorial data report aesthetic. Inspired by premium industry research reports — cold white background, deep navy and cold blue as the only colors, large bold numbers as visual anchors, strict grid layout, typographic hierarchy doing all the work. NOT hand-drawn. NOT warm. NO organic shapes. Feels like a McKinsey chart page or an Economist data spread — authoritative, cold, precise.

## Canvas & Colors
- Background: cold white #F5F7FA
- Primary accent: deep navy #1A3A7A (title, key numbers, section bars)
- Secondary accent: cold blue #2A5CAA (borders, chart fills, dividers)
- Light fill: ice blue #EBF0FA (chart backgrounds, subtle tints)
- Body text: dark blue-gray #1A1C2A
- Secondary text: slate #8A9AB0
- Divider lines: 1px cold blue #2A5CAA

## Typography Rules
- Large numbers: 44-48px, ultra-bold, deep navy — these are the visual anchors
- Title: 36-40px, ultra-bold, deep navy
- Section labels: 12px, bold small caps, letter-spacing 0.15em, cold blue
- Column headers: 11px, bold small caps
- Body: 11-12px regular
- ALL SANS-SERIF GEOMETRIC — Helvetica, Inter, or similar — NEVER handwritten or serif

## What Makes This Feel Premium
- The huge numbers stop the eye and create hierarchy
- White space between sections is as important as the content
- Every divider line is exactly the same weight
- Color palette has exactly 2 colors (navy + cold blue) — no exceptions

## Absolutely No
- Hand-drawn textures or sketch lines
- Warm colors of any kind
- Rounded organic blobs or speech bubbles  
- Decorative icons or illustrations
- Any element that adds "friendliness" — this card is authoritative
